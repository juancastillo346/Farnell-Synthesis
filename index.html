Farnell Synthesis Lab 3 Part I & II

Footsteps on Gravel Blog

This page documents my Web Audio versions of the two lab sounds: a translated babbling brook patch for Part I and a procedural footsteps-on-gravel sound for Part II.

Part I: Babbling Brook

For Part I, I translated the SuperCollider brook patch into Web Audio. The sound starts with Brown Noise, then uses filtering and slow cutoff modulation to make the texture feel uneven and watery.

  • Brown Noise acts as the raw water source.
  • OnePole smooths the main noise before filtering.
  • A low-pass filtered control signal moves the high-pass cutoff.

Part II: Footsteps on Gravel

For Part II, I followed Farnell’s idea of separating control from synthesis. Each step is broken into heel, edge, and ball phases, and those phases drive short filtered noise bursts to create a gravel texture.

  • The heel adds the strongest impact.
  • The edge phase carries the body weight across the ground.
  • The ball phase adds the push-off at the end of the step.
